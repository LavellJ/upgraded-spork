Build me a Quest Island learning map for kids that combines a gentle adventure theme with a calm and aesthetic style inspired by Alto’s Adventure.
Key Design Goals:
•	Style: Minimalist, soft gradients, pastel tones (sand, ocean blues, sunset purples, jungle greens). Avoid generic emoji or cartoon clipart.
•	Animations: Smooth, slow transitions (drifting clouds, flowing waves, glowing path). No bouncy or flashy animations.
•	Scout (guide): A small character who walks along the path, pauses, and points at landmarks. Think gentle excitement, not over-the-top.
•	Map layout: An island divided into biomes — beach, jungle, desert cliffs, volcano, lagoon. Each biome represents a subject area. A glowing winding path connects them.
•	Lesson markers: Glowing waypoints (stones, shells, lanterns). When completed, they softly light up.
•	Rewards: Collectibles are elegant (gems, feathers, shells) that go into a “Journey Journal.” Avoid childish coins or stars.
•	Immersion: Add subtle ambient elements (butterflies, drifting lanterns, fireflies, faint volcano smoke). The map should feel alive but peaceful.
•	Sound cues (optional): Gentle chimes or nature sounds tied to progress.
•	Responsiveness: Should scale well to tablet and desktop screens.
Deliverable:
Generate a clean, modular skeleton in React with TailwindCSS where:
•	The map is the main component.
•	Scout is a movable character with idle + pointing animations.
•	Each biome has placeholder lesson nodes (glowing circles).
•	Rewards trigger a modal/journal screen with collectibles.
•	Design is calm, immersive, and aesthetically cohesive.
Keep everything simple but structured so I can later add lesson content and audio (via ElevenLabs).
